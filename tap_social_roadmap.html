Engagement Framework

How to Drive Real Engagement

The tactics that consistently generate above-average engagement in the UK agricultural sector — grounded in what makes farming audiences actually respond.

🌾
Seasonal Content Calendar
Plan content around the farming year. Harvest (Aug–Oct), planting (Mar–May), shows (LAMMA Jan, Cereals June) and DEFRA policy moments create natural engagement peaks. Be ready to publish fast when they hit.
🎯
Show the Work, Not Just the Result
Farming audiences are practical. Show the brief, the process, the creative debate. "How we planned the BASF spring campaign" consistently outperforms "BASF campaign live now."
🤝
Engage Before You Post
Spend 20 minutes every morning commenting genuinely on posts from farmers, agronomists, NFU and trade bodies before publishing TAP content. Warms the algorithm and builds real relationships simultaneously.
🚁
Drone Footage Is Your Superpower
Very few marketing agencies have genuine aerial agricultural content capability. A 30-second drone field video at harvest, posted to Reels or TikTok, will consistently outperform any static content.
📊
Data-Led Posts on LinkedIn
"We analysed 50 UK agri-brand Instagram accounts — here's what works." Original data creates shareability, positions TAP as an authority, and generates enquiries from brands who want the full report.
🔗
Tag & Collaborate Strategically
Tag clients when celebrating their campaigns. Tag creators when their work inspires you. Tag industry bodies when reacting to news. This dramatically expands organic reach beyond TAP's own follower base.
💬
Ask Questions That Matter
End every post with a question the audience genuinely wants to answer: "What should UK farms be doing differently on social in 2026?" — not "What do you think? Let us know below!"
🏆
Celebrate Awards Loudly
TAP has Rural Business and PPA wins. These should be recurring content: anniversary posts, behind-the-scenes of the submission, and what the win means to the team — not a single announcement post.
📅
Consistency Beats Perfection
Three high-quality posts per week, every week, outperforms sporadic viral attempts. Build a content bank of 20+ posts before launch so publishing never stalls under capacity pressure.
